# Realmweaver: Star Forged — Lore Reference

## Purpose

This document is the worldbuilding reference for the **Star Forged** Hytale asset pack.
It is meant to guide asset creation, zone themes, factions, creatures, props, UI tone, item naming, and future quest writing.

This setting is **inspired by the energy of colorful frontier sci-fantasy** in the same broad space as games like WildStar, but it must remain **fully original** in names, factions, history, visuals, and terminology.

---

## High Concept

**Star Forged** is a high-energy sci-fantasy frontier setting built around the ruins of a shattered celestial forge-world.

Long ago, an ancient civilization known as the **Forge Choir** shaped living metal, starstone, and world-engines capable of terraforming entire regions. Their greatest creation, the planet **Veyra Null**, was a living forge designed to seed new worlds with life, power, and impossible materials.

Then the forge-world broke.

Its planetary lattice fractured, its orbital rings fell, and its buried engines awoke in pieces. Now Veyra Null is a wild patchwork of glowing deserts, jungle vaults, broken foundries, floating debris fields, buried machine temples, and unstable aether storms.

Modern factions have arrived to claim it:
- scavengers looking for salvage
- corporate charter houses seeking profit
- mystics who believe the world is alive
- raider clans born from the wasteland
- settlers trying to survive between ruins and monsters

The core fantasy is:

**explore a dangerous, vibrant, ancient world where lost super-technology feels like magic and every region hides something worth fighting over.**

## Core Setting Pillars

### 1. Bright frontier sci-fantasy
The world should feel adventurous, colorful, and bold rather than bleak hard sci-fi.

Keywords:
- vibrant
- dangerous
- punchy
- weird
- heroic
- treasure-hunter energy
- frontier colonies
- alien wilderness
- lost megastructures

### 2. Ancient technology that feels mythical
Old machines are not clean or sterile. They are monumental, ceremonial, and half-understood.

Keywords:
- star forges
- living metal
- reactor shrines
- echo cores
- world engines
- gravity spires
- bio-mechanical ruins

### 3. Civilization versus wildness
Settlements are fragile footholds. Outside the walls, the world is predatory, unstable, and full of opportunity.

Keywords:
- outposts
- rail camps
- prospectors
- defense turrets
- broken roads
- creature nests
- storm zones

### 4. Action-first tone
Everything should support mobility, combat readability, and strong silhouettes.

Keywords:
- oversized tools
- modular armor
- energized weapons
- readable enemy roles
- traversal hooks
- faction color coding

### 5. Layered mystery
The player should always feel there is a deeper truth under the surface.

Questions the setting should raise:
- Who really built the forge-world?
- Why did the Forge Choir vanish?
- Is Veyra Null broken, sleeping, or evolving?
- Are the ruins factories, temples, or both?
- Is the world calling people here on purpose?

## The World: Veyra Null

## Summary
Veyra Null is a broken forge-planet at the edge of mapped space. It was once a world-scale creation engine. After an ancient collapse called the **Shattering Pulse**, its biomes and machine systems split into unstable but resource-rich territories.

The planet now behaves like a machine trying to repair itself with damaged instructions.

## Known traits
- Star-metal veins erupt from the ground like crystalized steel roots.
- Buried engines periodically awaken and alter weather, gravity, heat, or local ecology.
- Ancient transit structures still function in fragments.
- Some wildlife has adapted to forge-radiance and developed metallic, luminous, or synthetic traits.
- Ruins often blend sacred symbolism with industrial design.

## Common world materials
These should recur in environment art, item descriptions, and crafting identity.

- **Star Iron** — dense metal with a faint blue internal glow
- **Aetherglass** — translucent mineral used in lenses, circuits, and relic blades
- **Pulse Copper** — conductive orange metal common in old machine sites
- **Bloomsteel** — living alloy that slowly reshapes itself under energy exposure
- **Ash Coral** — pale mineral growth found around dead reactors and dry seas
- **Gravemoss** — a soft bioluminescent plant that grows in low-gravity ruins

## The Ancient Civilization: The Forge Choir

## Public understanding
Most settlers know only fragments:
- they built impossible machines
- they blended engineering with ritual
- they could grow matter and shape terrain
- they vanished before any modern faction arrived

## Design identity
The Forge Choir should feel:
- elegant but massive
- ceremonial but functional
- mathematically precise with organic curvature
- built around rings, halos, channels, vaults, and radiant cores

## What they left behind
- fabrication temples
- dormant guardians
- adaptive tools
- memory obelisks
- atmosphere regulators
- buried transit gates
- forge-seeds that can reshape local terrain or life

## The mystery
The Forge Choir may not be extinct in a normal sense. The working theory for the setting is that parts of them were absorbed into the world-engine network, becoming distributed machine-intelligences called **Echoes**.

Echoes are not ghosts. They are incomplete personalities, commands, and instincts left inside the world.

This gives the setting:
- lore mystery
- AI shrine content
- voice-driven ruins
- corrupted machine dungeons
- factions trying to control ancient consciousness

## Primary Modern Factions

## 1. The Charter Concord
A coalition of licensed prospectors, corporate explorers, security outfits, and contracted engineers.

### Fantasy
Frontier capitalism with polished gear, branded equipment, survey drones, portable foundries, and heavily armed expedition teams.

### Goals
- secure profitable relic sites
- establish stable settlements
- map resource corridors
- monopolize high-value salvage
- control transit and shipping routes

### Visual direction
- white, brass, navy, orange accents
- modular armor and industrial backpacks
- survey beacons, crates, rail lines, scanners
- clean silhouettes with field-worn damage

### Mood
Pragmatic, ambitious, self-justifying.

## 2. The Ember Clades
Confederated wasteland clans shaped by exposure to forge-radiance and life on the planet's hostile surface.

### Fantasy
Nomadic raiders, beast riders, scrap shamans, survivalists, and fierce local powers who know the land better than outsiders.

### Goals
- defend territory from extraction fleets
- raid supply lines
- claim sacred machine sites
- harness wild forge energy without outside control

### Visual direction
- layered scrap, bone, hide, glowing paint
- salvaged tech fused with tribal ornament
- asymmetrical armor and trophy pieces
- banners, cages, spikes, mounted engines

### Mood
Ferocious, proud, adaptive, superstitious but not primitive.

## 3. The Astral Mendicants
Pilgrims, scholars, signal-listeners, and techno-mystics who believe Veyra Null is a wounded divine engine.

### Fantasy
Monastic explorers of sacred machinery who decode songs, pulses, and architectural alignments within the ruins.

### Goals
- commune with Echoes
- restore stable machine regions
- prevent reckless exploitation
- uncover the true purpose of the planet

### Visual direction
- cloth layered over tech frames
- teal, violet, silver, pale gold
- masks, antenna halos, tuning rods, shrine packs
- clean robes contrasted with machine relic implants

### Mood
Calm, unsettling, prophetic.

## 4. The Null Maw
A loose umbrella term for corrupted entities, rogue constructs, parasitic growths, and cultic warbands aligned with planetary malfunction.

### Fantasy
The broken immune system of the world and the opportunists who worship breakdown.

### Goals
- spread instability
- awaken sealed cores incorrectly
- corrupt relics and transit systems
- feed on energy surges and fear

### Visual direction
- blackened metal, magenta fractures, red-orange core wounds
- jagged geometry and exposed internals
- corrupted halos, twisted drones, unstable growths

### Mood
Predatory, feverish, uncanny.

## Settlement Types

These should guide prop sets and biome storytelling.

### Frontier landing camp
Temporary shelters, cargo stacks, landing pads, floodlights, fuel tanks, prefab walls.

### Salvage town
Built from reclaimed ship hulls and ruin fragments. Messy, vertical, entrepreneurial.

### Concord extraction post
Defensible industrial base with drills, conveyors, power rigs, scanners, and armored gates.

### Clade warcamp
Mobile-feeling settlement with beast pens, banners, scrap towers, cooking pits, ritual engines.

### Mendicant shrine enclave
Quiet settlement built around an ancient ruin node, with tuned chimes, data altars, meditation platforms, and protected gardens.

### Broken forge city
A semi-dungeon urban ruin from the ancient world. Monumental streets, inactive constructs, collapsed bridges, reactor cathedrals.

## Biome and Zone Themes

## 1. Sunflare Expanse
A red-gold desert scattered with cooling towers, half-buried railguns, and skeletal relay spires.

Use for:
- salvage zones
- raider territory
- open world traversal
- early frontier fantasy

## 2. Verdant Circuit
A jungle grown through machine ruins where roots, cables, and luminous water merge together.

Use for:
- overgrown temples
- wildlife-heavy content
- hidden labs and shrines
- exploration and vertical traversal

## 3. The Hollow Ring
A region beneath a collapsed orbital structure. Gravity is inconsistent, debris hangs in the air, and old defense systems still track movement.

Use for:
- platforming
- strange skyboxes
- relic vaults
- high-tech dungeons

## 4. Glasswake Coast
A shoreline where the sand fused into reflective glass after ancient energy discharge. Tide pools hold mineral growth, wreckage, and mutated life.

Use for:
- beautiful but dangerous coasts
- crystalline creatures
- atmospheric gathering zones
- pirate or scavenger content

## 5. Cinder Bloom
Volcanic badlands where forge energy leaks through fractures in the crust and metallic flora grows in the ash.

Use for:
- elite monster regions
- fire and molten visuals
- heavy harvesting zones
- dangerous mid-to-late game content

## 6. The Choir Below
Deep subterranean machine cathedrals and automated foundry vaults under the surface.

Use for:
- ancient dungeon content
- boss arenas
- lore reveals
- Echo encounters

## Creature Direction

Creatures should feel like the ecosystem adapted to ancient energy rather than standard fantasy monsters dropped into a sci-fi map.

## Creature families

### Scrapbeasts
Pack hunters with plated hides, scavenger instincts, and visible metal growth.

### Pulse Wasps
High-mobility flying threats that nest near active power conduits.

### Glassmaws
Crystalline shoreline predators with reflective armor and sonic shrieks.

### Cinder Stags
Large territorial herbivores with ember-lit antlers and mineral armor.

### Vault Sentinels
Ancient constructs that still perform security functions long after their original orders were lost.

### Bloom Wyrms
Burrowing serpentine creatures that feed on heat, mineral deposits, and old reactor runoff.

### Nullbound
Corrupted hybrids of machine, flesh, and environmental growth tied to system malfunctions.

## Tech, Magic, and Power Logic

This setting should treat advanced technology and arcane-feeling energy as the same continuum.

## Core power source
### Forge Radiance
A planetary energy field generated by world-engine systems deep below the crust.

Forge Radiance can:
- power relic technology
- mutate creatures and plant life
- recharge artifacts
- destabilize matter under extreme exposure
- act as a ritual or magical force for those who learn to tune it

## Practical rule
Characters in-world may use different language for the same thing:
- engineers call it power, flux, or current
- mystics call it song, radiance, or breath
- raiders call it fireblood or stormlight

All are describing related phenomena.

This keeps the setting sci-fantasy rather than separating magic and technology too rigidly.

## Artifact Categories

These categories are useful for items, props, and dungeon rewards.

### Survey-tech
Modern tools used by factions to scan, mine, and map the world.

### Salvaged gear
Rebuilt ancient parts repurposed into practical weapons and tools.

### Choir relics
Original ancient devices with elegant forms and partially unknown functions.

### Tuned relics
Artifacts altered by Mendicants or local tinkerers to interact with Echo systems.

### Corrupted relics
Unstable items touched by Null Maw influence.

## Naming Conventions

Use names that feel bold, punchy, and readable.

## Good naming patterns
- two-part names: **Sunflare Reach**, **Ironwake Basin**, **Halo March**
- material-forward names: **Star Iron**, **Aetherglass**, **Bloomsteel**
- dramatic functional names: **Vault Crown**, **Transit Spine**, **Forge Gate**
- factional terms: **Concord Ward**, **Clade Huntmaster**, **Mendicant Tuner**

## Avoid
- direct borrowing from existing franchises
- overly generic space opera names
- overly grimdark naming in every area
- naming everything with random apostrophes

When creating the pack, favor:
- chunky silhouettes
- strong color blocking
- readable faction palettes
- ancient-tech shapes mixed with frontier kitbashing
- glowing seams and energy vents
- ruined megastructures visible on horizons

### Environment prop examples
- cracked signal pylons
- portable refinery units
- shrine terminals
- salvage cranes
- hover carts
- beast hitch posts
- broken guardian heads

Music and sound should support a sense of frontier momentum and ancient wonder.

### Music feel
- driving percussion
- synth pulses
- adventurous motifs
- ambient choir textures for ancient sites
- desert, jungle, and ruin regional flavors

### Sound design feel
- heavy mechanical impacts
- humming cores
- electrical arcing
- tuned chimes in shrine spaces
- creature sounds that blend organic and synthetic qualities

---

## Guardrails for Originality

To keep Star Forged distinct:
- do not reuse names, factions, species, terminology, or signature props from other IPs
- do not clone a specific game's exact humor style or visual language
- keep the inspiration at the level of **energy and genre blend**, not direct content copying
- emphasize the broken forge-world concept as the setting's unique core idea
- root all major content in Veyra Null, the Forge Choir, Echoes, and Forge Radiance

## Asset Pack Reference Checklist

Use this checklist when designing new content.

Does it:
- feel adventurous, vibrant, and dangerous?
- support frontier sci-fantasy rather than hard sci-fi?
- include signs of ancient machine civilization?
- reinforce one of the major factions or biome identities?
- feel original rather than borrowed?
- visually support readable gameplay?
- hint at deeper mystery?

If yes, it likely fits Star Forged.
